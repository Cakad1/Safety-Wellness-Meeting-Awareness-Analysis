# Safety & Wellness Meeting Awareness Analysis

## Project Overview
This project analyzes survey data from 95 total respondents to evaluate the visibility and impact of the Safety & Wellness Office and its associated meetings. The goal is to identify gaps between employee awareness and actual attendance to improve future participation.

## Method
### Data Source

The project uses a **synthetic survey dataset containing 95 respondent records**. The dataset was generated to simulate employee responses related to awareness of the Safety & Wellness Office, meeting awareness, past attendance, barriers to attendance, and interest in future meetings.

### Data Preparation

The dataset was cleaned and prepared using **Power Query**. Data preparation included reviewing the dataset for consistency and organizing survey responses for analysis.

A **logic table** was used to connect related survey responses and classify respondents based on their awareness and attendance status. This supported the identification of groups such as ***Aware Attendees, Aware Non-attendees, Unaware Non-attendees,*** and ***Office Aware Non-attendees.***

### Tools Used

- **Microsoft Excel:** Used as the primary tool for data analysis and dashboard development.
- **Power Query:** Used to clean, transform, and prepare the survey data for analysis.
- **PivotTables:** Used to summarize survey responses and calculate key metrics.
- **Slicers:** Used to provide interactive filtering.
- **Excel Charts:** Used to visualize key findings.

### Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)
- **Total Respondents:** 95
- **Awareness of Office:** 74%
- **Awareness of Meeting:** 60%
- **Past 3-Year Attendance:** 31%
- **Interest in Future Meetings:** 69%
 
### Demographic Breakdown 
The data is categorized by various employee segments to understand the respondent profile:

- **By Department:** Participation was led by ***Administration (39)***, followed by ***Sales & Marketing (36)***, and ***Repairs (20).***
- **Years of Service:** Ranges included under ***1 year, 1–5 years, 6–10 years, 11–20 years, 21–30 years,*** and ***31–40 years.***
- **Supervisor Status:** Data includes both ***Supervisors*** and ***Non-supervisors.***
 
### Awareness VS. Attendance Insights
The analysis reveals a significant drop-off from awareness to participation:

**Awareness Levels:** While ***60%*** of respondents are aware of both the office and the meetings, ***26%*** are completely unaware of both.

**Attendance Status:**
- **Aware Attendees:** 31% (29 individuals).
- **Aware Non-attendees:** 29% (28 individuals).
- **Unaware Non-attendees:** 26%.
- **Office Aware Non-attendees:** 14% (13 individuals).
 
### Barriers to Attendance
To improve future attendance, the project identified the primary reasons for non-participation:

- **Unaware:** 42% of non-attendees cite a lack of awareness as the primary barrier.
- **Time Conflict:** 23% of respondents are unable to attend due to scheduling issues.
- **NA (Not Applicable):** 35%.
 
### Future Outlook
There is a strong potential for growth in program engagement, as 69% of respondents expressed a definitive interest in attending future meetings, despite the current attendance rate being only 31%.

### Key Insights
- **Awareness-to-Attendance Gap:** Meeting awareness stands at 60%, while past three-year attendance is only 31%.
- **Awareness Barrier:** Lack of awareness accounts for 42% of identified non-attendance barriers.
- **High Future Interest:** 69% of respondents expressed interest in attending future meetings.
- **Scheduling Challenges:** Time conflicts account for 23% of identified attendance barriers.

## Conclusion

The analysis identified a clear gap between employee awareness and actual participation in Safety & Wellness meetings. Although 60% of respondents are aware of the meetings, only 31% attended within the past three years.

Lack of awareness and scheduling conflicts were identified as key barriers to participation. However, the 69% interest in future meetings indicates strong potential for increased employee engagement.

Overall, the findings suggest that improving communication, meeting accessibility, and targeted employee outreach could help convert employee interest into actual meeting attendance.
 
## Recommendations
- **Increase Communication:** Address the 26% of employees who are completely unaware of the office and meetings to bridge the awareness gap.
- **Flexible Scheduling:** Resolve time conflicts (affecting 23% of respondents) by offering meetings at varying times or through recorded sessions.
- **Targeted Outreach:** Focus on the ***"Aware Non-attendees"*** (29%) who already know about the meetings but have yet to participate.
